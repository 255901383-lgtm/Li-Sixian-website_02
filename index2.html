Li Sixian, How Not to Be Heard: A Survival Manual for Sound in the Digital Age, 2025

This film is inspired by Hito Steyerl's How Not to Be Seen: A Fucking Didactic Educational .MOV File (2013), and uses performance, green screen keying, and animation to explore the contemporary condition of sound in the digital age — particularly the tension between voice, data, and surveillance.

Be careful — you might already be overheard.

Do we still live in a world of real sound, or one of digitally replicated audio?

What happens when voices are cloned — is it still your voice, or someone else's property?

Can you still say your words represent you?

Are our digital doubles gradually replacing us?

Perhaps this Survival Manual for Sound will help you avoid being heard — or help you realize how impossible that already is.
